Coffee Cherries
The Bean

    Coffee is technically not a bean. It is actually a seed! The plant on which coffee cherries grow originated in Ethiopia. From there it spread through trade and culture. It is now cultivated in over 70 countries. What makes the plant so special is that in each of these countries, the coffee can taste widely different. There are now reported to be over 100 different coffee species. Each having been derived through natural selection or selectively breed for their unique qualities.

    When you first begin to try different coffees, it's sometimes easier to make the distinction between different origins rather than coffee variety. Varieties will have their own unique flavor, but the distinction between origin is usually more noticable to the palete. Process

    The process in which coffee cherries go through after they are picked is also very important when it comes to flavor. Natural or dry-processed coffee is when the cherries are left to dry in the sun for several weeks. This allows the fruit of the cherry to seep into the seed imparting a lasting flavor of the fruit around it. Natural processed coffees tend to be fruity and sweet. Washed process on the other hand, involves heavily washing the fruit off of the seed after it has been picked. This tends to give the consumer more insight on the flavor of the coffee seed itself, which can vary region to region.
